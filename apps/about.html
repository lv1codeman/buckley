About me

Pianist Kaili Ho has been performing throughout the United States, Asia, and Europe from a very young age. Ms. Ho made her professional solo debut at the age of 6 at Taiwan’s National Concert Hall and has held many solo recitals around US, Taiwan, and China since then. Ms. Ho has appeared with orchestras including the Orchestre des Champs-Élysées, Vidin State Philharmonic Orchestra, the Colburn Academy Virtuosi Orchestra, and the National University of Singapore Orchestra. Ms. Ho was sought out by the Taiwanese government to perform at the Taiwan Art Vision Festival in 2006. Furthermore, she was a Grand Prize winner for The Metropolitan International Piano Competition to perform at the Weill Concert Hall of Carnegie Hall in New York City.

Ms. Ho has been a recipient of numerous awards, including First prize at the MTNA International State Piano Competition, First Prize at the Kawai International Piano Competition, “Best Performance of Taiwanese Repertoire” at the Young Musician International Competition - Citta di Barletta, Second Prize at the Taiwan National Piano Competition, and Third Prize at the Virtuoso and Bel Canto Music Competition in Italy. She was also selected as one of the top ten pianists to participate in the Ninth Moscow International Frederick Chopin Competition for Young Pianists.

Ms. Ho has attended many summer music festivals including The Colburn Piano Festival in Los Angeles, Art of the piano Festival at Cincinnati Conservatory of Music, Engadin International Piano Festival in Switzerland, Donghai University Music Festival in Taiwan, Virtuoso and Bel Canto Music Festival in Italy, and MCL Summer Music Festival in Chicago. Ms. Ho has also participated in many piano and chamber music masterclasses throughout her studies, with renowned musicians such as Leon Fleisher, Jerome Lowenthal, Boris Berman, Arnold Steinhardt, Meng-Chieh Liu, Seymour Lipkin, Ning An, Roger Vignoles, and Matti Raekallio.

Raised in Kaohsiung, Taiwan, Ms. Ho received full scholarship studying at The Colburn School in Los Angeles. She received another four-year full scholarship for her Joint Honors Bachelor of Music between National University of Singapore and The Peabody Institute of The Johns Hopkins University and is currently at Peabody for Master of Music Piano Performance with Full Assistantship, where she collaborates regularly with lots of instrumental and vocal recitals, under the tutelage of Marian Hahn.
